Steps to Make Bisi bele Bath

  1. Ingredients

    • 1 cup raw rice (preferably Sona Masoori or Ponni)
    • ½ cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)
    • 4 cups water
    • ½ tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp oil or ghee

  2. For the sambar base:

    • 1 tbsp oil
    • 1 medium onion, chopped, or 6–8 shallots
    • 1 medium tomato, chopped
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, pumpkin, potato, etc.), chopped
    • 2 tsp sambar powder
    • ¼ tsp asafoetida
    • 1 tsp thick tamarind extract
    • Salt, to taste

  3. For the final tempering:

    • 2 tsp ghee
    • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • 2–3 dried red chilies
    • 1 sprig curry leaves
    • A pinch of asafoetida
    • A handful of cashews

  4. Instructions

    1. Pressure cook rice and dal: Thoroughly rinse the rice and toor dal. Add them to a pressure cooker along with 4 cups of water, turmeric powder, and a teaspoon of oil or ghee. Cook for 4–5 whistles on a medium flame
    2. Prepare the vegetables: While the rice and dal are cooking, heat a tablespoon of oil in a separate pan. Add the chopped onions or shallots and sauté until translucent. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
    3. Add spices and vegetables: Add the mixed vegetables, sambar powder, and asafoetida. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell of the spices disappears. Add the tamarind extract and bring the mixture to a boil. Simmer for 5–7 minutes, or until the vegetables are cooked through and the raw flavor of the tamarind has gone.
    4. Combine and simmer: Once the pressure has naturally released from the cooker, open it and gently mash the cooked rice and dal. Add this mashed mixture to the pan with the vegetable-sambar base. Mix well and adjust the consistency by adding hot water as needed. Remember that sambar rice thickens as it cools
    5. Prepare the final tempering: In a small frying pan, heat the ghee. Add the mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add the dried red chilies, curry leaves, and a pinch of asafoetida, then stir for a few seconds. If using, add the cashews and fry until golden.
    6. Finish and serve: Pour the tempering over the sambar rice and mix it in gently. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a drizzle of extra ghee if you like. Serve hot with a side of papad, chips, or pickle.
