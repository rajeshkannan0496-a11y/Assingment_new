Apple is one of the most popular and loved fruits in the world. It is round in shape and comes in various colors like red, green, and yellow. Apples have a sweet or slightly sour taste depending on the type. They belong to the Rosaceae family, and their scientific name is *Malus domestica*. Apples grow on trees that are mainly found in cool and temperate regions. The fruit has smooth skin and a juicy inside, with small brown seeds in the center. Apples are rich in vitamins and minerals, especially Vitamin C and dietary fiber, which help boost immunity and support digestion. The saying “An apple a day keeps the doctor away” reminds us of its great health benefits. Apples are low in calories, making them a healthy snack for everyone. There are thousands of apple varieties found across the world. Some popular types include Fuji, Gala, Red Delicious, and Granny Smith. Fuji and Gala

Apples can be eaten raw, or used in various dishes like pies, salads, jams, and juices. Apple pie and apple juice are loved by people of all ages. Dried apples and apple cider are also popular products. Apple trees bloom in spring with beautiful pink and white flowers. Bees play an important role in pollinating these flowers. After pollination, the fruit grows and ripens during the summer, and the apples are harvested in autumn. Apple trees can live for many years and produce fruit season after season. The top apple-producing countries are China, the United States, and India. In India, apples are mostly grown in the hilly regions of Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, and Uttarakhand. Apples grow best in well-drained soil and need plenty of sunlight. They are a symbol of health, freshness, and beauty. The fruit is not only delicious but also highly nutritious, filled with antioxidants and water that keep the body hydrated. Eating apples regularly helps improve heart health, reduces the risk of diabetes, and promotes glowing skin. Truly, the apple is a gift of nature — tasty, healthy, and refreshing, making it one of the most loved fruits on Earth.
